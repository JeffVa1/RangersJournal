In the shadow of a tyrant’s rule, where fear is currency and silence is survival, a single spark was struck in the dim light of a slumside tavern.
It began not with heroes, but with witnesses—strangers drawn together by defiance, by bloodshed, and by a choice that could not be undone. When a man dared to speak against the God-King, the world answered with steel and fire. A boy fell. A friend was left behind. And in the chaos, a path was chosen—one that led away from safety and into the dark places of the world.
Through tangled forests and forgotten roads, the fledgling companions were tested. They faced the quiet cruelty of the wild, the cunning of goblins, and the weight of a destiny whispered through divine light. Beneath the earth, in the buried halls of Irillis, they unearthed more than relics—they awakened something ancient. A truth sealed in blood. A power that would not remain hidden.
The Lost Scrolls were never meant to be found.
And yet, they were.
What followed was not triumph, but consequence. The dead did not stay dead. The God-King did not look away. And as the party emerged from the crypt, they carried with them a fragile, dangerous hope—the kind that draws the gaze of kings and monsters alike.
In the holy city of Brightmarch, that hope ignited into chaos.
Water turned to corruption. The streets whispered of disappearances. Illusions gave way to revelation as enemies moved unseen among the living. Betrayal wore a familiar face. And when steel clashed beneath the ringing bells of alarm, the companions stood at the center of it all—outmatched, hunted, and unwilling to yield.
An inquisitor fell.
But the cost was written in fire, in blood, and in the knowledge that this was only the beginning.
For now the world has taken notice.
The God-King’s gaze has found them.
And the chains of this age have begun to crack.
