The world does not whisper anymore, it listens. Beneath the iron rule of a God King who has crushed hope into dust, even the act of speaking feels like treason. In the rotting corners of Drakensport, where desperation clings to every breath, a spark of rebellion is snuffed out almost as soon as it ignites. Hammond’s cry for defiance ends in blood, and from that massacre rises something broken, something bound not by loyalty, but by survival. They are strangers stitched together by loss, hunted before they understand why, and already marked by forces far greater than themselves. They did not choose this path… but fate chose them.

Now, searching for the Lost Scroll of Irillis dangling like a curse in their hands, they are dragged deeper into a world unraveling at the seams. Creatures twist into unnatural forms, ancient crypts breathe with something old and hungry, and the God King’s reach stretches farther than any shadow should. Betrayal festers close, prophecy coils tighter, and the name of The Deathless One lingers like a promise no one survives. What began as escape has become something far more dangerous, an awakening. And as they carve their way through ruin and revelation, one truth settles in like a blade between the ribs: they are not just being hunted… they are being watched.
